Overview

The patents listed below reflect my effort to advance machine learning methods that are both statistically principled and practically effective. Much of this work centers on the development and application of non-parametric Bayesian models — particularly the Hierarchical Pitman-Yor Process (HPYP) — to extract structure from complex, sparse, and noisy data sources such as call transcripts, electronic documents, and genomic sequences.

Several of these inventions introduce novel ways to incorporate domain knowledge into probabilistic models through mixture priors, enabling faster training, better generalization, and greater interpretability — especially in low-data regimes. Others address key challenges in robust classification, such as label noise and model calibration, with solutions tailored for high-stakes or resource-constrained environments. The importance of sparse representations and identifiable features for interpretability, trustworthiness, and generalization is another consistent theme of my work.

Together, these patents lay the groundwork for scalable, interpretable AI systems that retain statistical rigor while adapting flexibly to the needs of real-world applications. They represent both theoretical innovation and applied insight across fields as diverse as natural language processing, semantic search, and biomedical signal detection.
